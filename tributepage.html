Elon Musk  



Early Life

At just 12 years old, he created and sold a video game called Blastar.Elon Reeve Musk is a visionary entrepreneur, engineer, and inventor known for his groundbreaking contributions including space exploration, electric vehicles, artificial intelligence, and renewable energy. Born on June 28, 1971, in Pretoria, South Africa, Musk developed an early interest in technology and went on to study physics and economics at the University of Pennsylvania. He then launched X.com, which evolved into PayPal and was acquired by eBay for $1.5 billion.

  
Joined Tesla
In 2004, he joined Tesla, transforming it into the leading electric vehicle manufacturer with innovations in battery technology and autonomous driving. Musk also co-founded Neuralink, which develops brain-computer interfaces, and The Boring Company, focused on tunnel-based transportation solutions. His acquisition of Twitter in 2022, later rebranded as X, was part of his vision to create an "everything app. " Despite facing controversies, legal challenges, and project delays, Musk continues to push the boundaries of technology, advocating for AI safety, sustainable energy, and interplanetary exploration.
  
SpaceX
In 2002, Musk founded SpaceX with the ambitious goal of making space travel affordable and enabling human colonization of Mars. His involvement with Tesla, beginning in 2004, transformed the electric vehicle market, leading to mass production of EVs and advancements in battery technology and self-driving systems. He also established The Boring Company, which focuses on tunnel infrastructure and reducing urban congestion.
  
Twitter
In 2022, Musk acquired Twitter (now rebranded as X) for $44 billion, aiming to turn it into a comprehensive platform for communication, payments, and artificial intelligence integration. Despite facing legal battles, business challenges, and public controversies, Musk remains committed to innovation, pushing humanity toward a future where space travel, AI, and clean energy are integral to daily life.
